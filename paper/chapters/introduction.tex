\section{INTRODUCTION}
\label{sec:intro}

The International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), founded in 1944 as a specialized agency of the United Nations, is tasked with promoting the safe and orderly development of international civil aviation. In its pursuit of a more efficient global air transport network, ICAO advocates for performance-based management, encouraging stakeholders to engage in comparative performance benchmarking to monitor and evolve the Air Traffic Management (ATM) community \cite{decea_mil}. At a strategic level, the Global Air Navigation Plan (GANP) maps the objectives required to accommodate all airspace users safely and economically while reducing the environmental footprint of aviation—directly supporting the United Nations' 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development \cite{doc9750}.

In this performance-oriented context, ICAO recommends using Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to help prioritize investments, identify bottlenecks, and disseminate best practices regionally and globally. In Brazil, the ATM Performance Commission (CP-ATM) supports the Department of Airspace Control (DECEA) by defining indicators and conducting post-operational analyses of the Brazilian Airspace Control System (SISCEAB) \cite{mca10022}. These KPIs represent fundamental strategies for improving governance in a highly regulated sector, where measuring outcomes such as punctuality, airport capacity, and fuel consumption allows operators and regulators to share strategic goals and mitigate environmental impacts.

This paper proposes the development of a machine learning algorithm to estimate the additional fuel consumption of aircraft—specifically addressing the metric known as KPI16—based on the aircraft model, departure and destination airports, and initial trajectory data within the Terminal Maneuvering Area (TMA). Accurately estimating this excess fuel burn can support analyses of operational efficiency, aid decisions regarding optimized approach procedures, and contribute to policies aimed at reducing atmospheric emissions \cite{baklacioglu2021fuel}.

The data utilized in this study consist of operational flight records and radar trajectories, aiming to provide a data-driven tool that continuous performance monitoring and operational improvements based on real-world scenarios.
