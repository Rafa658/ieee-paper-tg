\section{INTRODUCTION}
\label{sec:intro}

The International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), founded in 1944 as a specialized agency of the United Nations, is tasked with promoting the safe and orderly development of international civil aviation. In its pursuit of a more efficient global air transport network, ICAO advocates for performance-based management. This strategy encourages stakeholders to engage in comparative performance benchmarking to monitor and evolve the Air Traffic Management (ATM) community \cite{decea_mil}. At a strategic level, the Global Air Navigation Plan (GANP) maps the objectives required to accommodate all airspace users safely and economically, and simultaneously reduces the environmental footprint of aviation—directly supporting the United Nations' 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development \cite{doc9750}.

In this performance-oriented context, ICAO recommends using Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to help prioritize investments, identify bottlenecks, and disseminate best practices regionally and globally. In Brazil, the ATM Performance Commission (CP-ATM) supports the Department of Airspace Control (DECEA) by defining indicators and conducting post-operational analyses of the Brazilian Airspace Control System (SISCEAB) \cite{mca10022}. These KPIs represent fundamental strategies for improving governance in a highly regulated sector. Measuring outcomes such as punctuality, airport capacity, and fuel consumption allows operators and regulators to share strategic goals and mitigate environmental impacts.

In this paper, we propose the development of a machine learning framework to estimate the additional fuel consumption of aircraft in the Terminal Maneuvering Area (TMA). Specifically, we address the metric known as KPI16 by using the aircraft model, the departure and destination airports, and the initial trajectory data. Accurately estimating this excess fuel burn can support analyses of operational efficiency, aid decisions regarding optimized approach procedures, and contribute to policies aimed at reducing atmospheric emissions \cite{baklacioglu2021fuel}.

The data utilized in this study consist of operational flight records and radar trajectories. By combining these heterogeneous sources, we provide a data-driven tool that enables continuous performance monitoring and operational improvements based on real-world scenarios.
